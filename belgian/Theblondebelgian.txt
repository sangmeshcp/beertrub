HOME BREW RECIPE:
Title: The blonde belgian
Author: Sripathy

Brew Method: BIAB
Style Name: Witbier
Boil Time: 60 min
Batch Size: 4.5 gallons (fermentor volume)
Boil Size: 4 gallons
Boil Gravity: 1.071
Efficiency: 70% (brew house)


STATS:
Original Gravity: 1.063
Final Gravity: 1.013
ABV (standard): 6.6%
IBU (tinseth): 24.49
SRM (morey): 6.57

FERMENTABLES:
9 lb - Belgian - Pilsner (84.7%)
10 oz - Belgian - Aromatic (5.9%)
0.5 lb - Belgian - Wheat (4.7%)
0.5 lb - Cane Sugar (4.7%)

HOPS:
1.4 oz - Kent Goldings, Type: Pellet, AA: 5, Use: Boil for 50 min, IBU: 24.49

MASH GUIDELINES:
1) Temperature, Temp: 150 F, Time: 90 min, Mash with bag.

OTHER INGREDIENTS:
3 oz - Peaches, Time: 2880 min, Type: Other, Use: Secondary

YEAST:
White Labs - Trappist Ale Yeast WLP500
Starter: No
Form: Liquid
Attenuation (avg): 77.5%
Flocculation: Med-Low
Optimum Temp: 65 - 72 F
Fermentation Temp: 68 F


This recipe has been published online at:
http://www.brewersfriend.com/homebrew/recipe/view/379684/the-blonde-belgian


Brew day notes:

MASH:
1. Pre boiled water to strike temp of 160.
2. Added grain, saw the mash temp fall to 140 with approx 12 lbs of grain on 4 gallons of water at
160F
3. Pre boil OG was 1075.

BOIL:
1. Added 1/2 lbs of cane sugar to wort, and started boil @ 11.53 am.
2. At 12:03 (10 min) into boil, added 1 oz of kent goldings.
3. Measured G to be at 1080
4. @ 12:30 added more cane sugar ~ 1/2 lb. 
5. G was at 1085.
6. At 12:38 pm added 1 whirlfloc tablet.

COOLING:
1. Boil ended at 12:53. 
2. Wort was at 3 Gallons.
3. Added 1 gallon of mineral water sitting in fridge.
4. Cooled down to 70F @ 245 pm. So, it took 1 hour 45 mins to cool 4 gallons of wort from 150F to
70F.


FERMENTATION:
1. Pitched 2 packs of WLP550 Abbey Ale yeast pure pitch packs.
